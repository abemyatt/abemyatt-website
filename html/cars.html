My Car Ownership History

Mazda 3 Sport 2.0L NA Petrol (2019)

This was purchased to be a reliable, long-term ownership car. It's got a very premium feel to it, despite being a lot cheaper than competitive German cars. It's heavy, but solid.

It has a great balance of modern technology, without the annoying EU regulations which I think hamper the modern driving experience. The Mazda doesn't tell me off every time I slightly cross a lane, it doesn't try to throw me into a hedge on a single track lane because it's confused, and it remembers when I turn a setting off.

I plan to keep this car for as long as realistically possible, as it does everything I need and it does it well. Is it punchy, sporty, or fast? Not really. It has a 2.0L engine, but no turbo. Is it fuel efficient? Not really, but it's not a super gas guzzler either. I can get around 40MPG when driving around towns, and about 55MPG when doing long-distance motorway and dual-carriageway journeys.

Vauxhall Corsa C Sxi Twinport 1.4L Petrol (2005)

This car is pointless, but I own it. I bought it for £500 as a 'cheap run around'. It was dirty, smelled like dog, had near-naked tyres, was missing floor mats, had a mouldy and broken driver side sun visor, bonnet rusting and lacquer peel, the list goes on...

For some reason, I've owned the car for a year and a half now, despite owning another more modern, more functional, and more reliable car. I'm using this Corsa as a way to learn about cars, servicing, maintenance, and repairs. I also find it fun to drive, despite it just being a shitbox.

Toyota Yaris Design 1.5L Petrol Hybrid (2024)

I bought this because I liked the colour, the interior looked nicer than my previous one, and I thought I'd treat myself after a payrise.

I regret this car though, as I had a few different issues. Initially, the windscreen had scratching which looked like it had been done when polishing. It wasn't fixable and needed the screen replaced. It was a brand new car. Also, it had all the annoying EU regulatory safety features, lane keep-assist which would automatically turn on whenever the car started and now needed 3 menus to disable, screaming "BRAKE" at me anytime I had to go around a corner passed parked cars, etc. It also had wireless apple car play, which people would say is a good feature, but it had terrible latency and even though I plugged in via USB, it still had this latency. On a drive to north wales, I lost count at the amount of times the lane assist tried harshly steering me into hedges. I had forgotten to turn it back off after a pitstop, and I almost got launched into a verge and hedge. Really annoying, and crazy that this is a regulatory 'safety' feature...

I got rid of it after 3 months as I really just didn't enjoy it, and it was too expensive to justify the experience (which was poor to be honest). I had the idea then to go back to an old cheap run around car, as this modern expensive one just put a sour taste in my mouth.

Toyota Yaris Design 1.5L Petrol Hybrid (2022)

I really liked this car, as it did everything it needed to do. It was extremely fuel efficient, having an average of 65MPG on everyday use. I liked all the modern features, and even though I needed to turn off lane keep assist every time, it was just a case of holding a button for 3 seconds after startup which I instinctively did every time I got in.

It was a decent price at the time, but I felt like Toyota were and have since been getting more expensive than it's worth.

Toyota Aygo X-Trend 1.0L Petrol(2020)

This was my second car, and my first modern car. I was shocked how comfortable it could be to drive even a small car, as my previous one was rough. The modern features felt great to me as someone who had just gone from a 2004 Peugeot 206. I had one issue though: Hills. This car could literally not go up a hill without it needing to be in 1st or 2nd gear, even a slight incline on a dual carriageway would slow it down unless I dropped a few gears. I don't mind changing down gears, but even after that it still didn't have much punch up the hill. It was even worse if I had passengers.

Peugeot 206, 1.1L Petrol (2004)

I couldn't tell you the trim, but I assume it was the lowest. It was an old, rough car, but the interior had a really nice look to it, with a blue accent on the seats and trims. It was my first car, costing a grand total of £420. My father test drove it, because I still didn't have my license at this point. He said it was fine, but after I passed my test and drove it, the alternator packed in. Luckily my brother repaired it for me with a second hand one for £36, bargain!

I loved this car, despite all the issues. Water would get into the front passenger footwell and soak everything, the sills were starting to rust away, the steering shook at 60+mph, the exhaust back box tried to fall off... but despite all this, it was my first car and always got me where I needed to be. I didn't know anything about car ownership or maintenance, so I naively didn't service it, didn't actively repair it or replace consumables. I just drove it. But it was perfect for that, it's all I needed it to do.
